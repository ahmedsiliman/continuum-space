The site is connected with Berlin city via railway, 
which gives an opportunity for a lifestyle that is highly 
connected with urban culture and off-city. 
Near the site Tesla Gigafactory is located, which 
contributed a lot to the problem of deforestation and 
groundwater quality/toxication. 
To the north from the site there is a Natural Reserve. 
Therefore, the site could become a bridge between 
natural and monoculture forest.

The project site was meticulously selected based on a comprehensive regi
onal analysis. It is strategically situated in close proximity to Berlin, offering 
unparalleled connectivity and accessibility. The site‘s northern boundary 
directly abuts the (national park) territories, establishing it as a vital bridge 
between the pristine natural forests and the existing monoculture forests. 
This strategic positioning not only enhances the project‘s ecological integ
rity but also serves as a testament to our commitment to sustainable de
velopment.

At the heart of this project lies the groundbreaking concept of a cybernetic 
forest habitat. This transformative approach seamlessly integrates human 
habitation within the natural ecosystem, fostering a harmonious coexisten
ce between the built and the natural world. The cybernetic forest habitat 
concept is driven by a deep understanding of the delicate balance of the 
forest ecosystem, ensuring that every aspect of the project complements 
and enhances the existing natural environment.

This project is not solely the brainchild of designers and architects; it is an 
integral collaboration involving diverse stakeholders, including scientists, 
ecologists, community leaders, and residents. This collaborative approach 
ensures that the project is not only ecologically sound but also socially and 
culturally sensitive, reflecting the needs and aspirations of the local com
munity.

The development of the project will follow a phased approach, carefully 
orchestrated to minimize disturbance to the existing ecosystem. The mas
terplan is divided into four stages: Cybernetic Analysis: a comprehensive 
assessment of the site‘s ecological characteristics. Zoning: Based on the 
cybernetic analysis, the site will be zoned to optimize the integration of hu
man habitation within the natural environment. Framework: This stage will 
establish the project‘s infrastructure, including roads, pathways, and utility 
networks. Dwelling: This final stage will involve the construction of dwel
lings that embody the cybernetic forest habitat concept.
